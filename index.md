## About Me


![Profile Picture]({{ "assets/images/profile-pic.jpg" | relative_url }}){: class="profile-image"}

 
My name is {{ site.name }}, and I am a Mechanical Engineering student (minor in Business and Engineering Management) at Cornell University who enjoys understanding how things work and turning ideas into real, tangible designs. I’ve had the opportunity to work on projects that span the full development process—from early concepts and prototypes to analysis and refinement—which has given me a practical perspective on engineering. Through my coursework and projects, I’ve built a strong foundation in areas like fluid mechanics, heat transfer, and mechanics of materials, but what I enjoy most is applying those principles to real problems. I like the process of taking something complex, breaking it down, and figuring out how to design a better or more efficient solution.

At the same time, I’m curious about the many directions mechanical engineering can take, and I don’t want to limit myself to just one area. I enjoy learning new tools, exploring different fields, and seeing how engineering connects across disciplines. Outside of academics, I spend a lot of time snowboarding, taking photos, and welding—things that keep me creative and hands-on. Whether it’s building something, capturing a moment, or learning a new skill, I’m always drawn to experiences that involve making, exploring, and understanding the world a little better.
